Enterprise (commonly called as business and used interchangeably) and society as brother and sister have been coeval since time immemorial. Both have been inter-related and inter-dependent in one way or other. Only the nature of interdependence between the two has, perhaps, changed over the period. There is no single definition of CSR universally accepted worldwide. Different people and organizations have defined CSR differently. Keith Davis (Davis 1960) defined social responsibility of business as: “The businessman’s decisions and actions taken for reasons at least partially beyond the firm’s direct economic or technical interest. In brief, corporate social responsibility means business’s responsiveness to the social causes of a given point of time.” Nonetheless, CSR is often described as a voluntary responsibility above and beyond the demands of national legislation and which encompasses issues such as human rights, environmental responsibility, and civic freedoms. One way of defining CSR is to look at the sustainable development of society as its starting point. This means let the business undertake efforts to unify economic, social, and environmental development.These efforts encompass every section of society.
